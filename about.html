Hi, I'm Rohan Walia


Ph.D. Student
Robotics Engineering
Worcester Polytechnic Institute


I am a Ph.D. student in the Robotics Engineering department at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, working at Automata Lab. My research focuses on safe control for learning-enabled AI systems in collaboration with NASA, and deadlock avoidance for multi-goal navigation in partnership with Amazon Robotics for warehouse applications.

Prior to my Ph.D., I worked as a Robotics Software Engineer at MathWorks (2022-2023), where I benchmarked motion planning algorithms and integrated deep learning networks for surface reconstruction. I also worked at BLACK-i Robotics (2024), developing automated 3D object modeling pipelines for robotic grasping using point cloud processing.

I hold a Master's degree in Robotics Engineering from WPI (2022) with a 4.0 GPA, and a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science from Virginia Tech (2020). During my studies, I completed an internship at Outrider Technologies and served as Operations Team Lead for NASA Space Robotics Challenge.
