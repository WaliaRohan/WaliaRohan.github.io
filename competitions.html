Some of the competitions I have participated in

Working in a team has always been a large part of my life. I have participated in multiple competitions, and have lead my team in several of them. An icon indicates that a competition video is available.

NASA Space Robotics Challenge Phase II



Role: Operations Subteam Lead

As the Operations Team Lead for the NASA Space Robotics Challenge, I led the development of motion control software for a simulated space robotics platform. I formulated an analytical inverse kinematics solution for a 4-DOF robotic arm, tuned PID controllers, and implemented trapezoidal velocity profiles to achieve smooth and reliable joint motion. I also designed and implemented high-level motion primitives to stream joint commands and execute planned trajectories using ROS, C++, and Linux.

AgBOT 2019



Role: Low-level Controls Subteam Lead

As part of Virginia Tech's 35-member AgBOT team, I led low-level controls development for an autonomous soil sampling system that won first place at Purdue University's 4th annual AgBOT competition. I developed test scripts to track trajectory data for a 4-degree-of-freedom robotic arm that automated sample collection, centrifuge operation, and pipetting for microscope analysis. To ensure reliable operation, I implemented isolated ground planes to eliminate electrical noise between high-power drivetrain systems and sensitive control circuits, and studied H-bridge motor control configurations for bidirectional operation of onboard motors. Our vehicle was the only entry to complete all competition requirements, contributing to a system that reduced traditional soil testing from weeks to approximately 20 minutes per sample. Participation in this competition got me into Robotics!
