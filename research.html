2025 - Present



Safe and fast multi-goal control with CBF-CLF QPs

Quadratic Programs (QPs) with Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) and Control Lyapunov Functions (CLFs) are widely used to implement safe, optimization-based control. However, when multiple goals and conflicting constraints are active, CBF-CLF QPs can introduce undesirable equilibria and even deadlocks in control and navigation. In this project, I study how degree of conflict relates to performance cost. I am developing a strategy that preemptively switches goals to avoid high-conflict regions and reduce performance degradation. I benchmark the approach across scenarios with varying degrees of conflict against alternative strategies.

2024 - 2025



Safe and flexible control for Learning Enabled Sensors


Engineering Reliable Autonomous Systems (ERAS) 2025
American Controls Conference (ACC) 2026

Learning-enabled perception components introduce state-dependent uncertainty that challenges safety-critical control. This work integrates principled models of perception error with estimation theory by extending the Belief Control Barrier Function framework using a Generalized Extended Kalman Filter (GEKF). The resulting approach reduces conservatism compared to fixed worst-case noise bounds while preserving safety, and is validated on 1D and 2D control tasks. Current iteration of this work is being validated on sim-to-real and real hardware settings.

Formation-Centric Software Interface


Masterâ€™s Thesis

This thesis presents an intuitive software interface for creating, managing, and navigating robot formations without manual per-robot programming. The framework introduces formation-aware data types and algorithms that enable rapid robot-to-formation assignment, dynamic shape changes, and rigid formation navigation in mapped environments. The approach is validated through simulated and real multi-robot experiments such as collective object transport, demonstrating scalable formation control for collective transport and coordinated motion tasks.
